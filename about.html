Utkarsh Kumar

Advocate · Founder, Amagi Legal

A litigation advocate whose practice is built on one conviction: that the quality of thought before the argument determines everything that follows.

Utkarsh Kumar is a graduate of Dr. Ram Manohar Lohiya National Law University, Lucknow (B.A. LL.B with Honours in Business Laws, 2021), one of India's foremost national law schools. He has been in practice since graduation and has built a substantive multi-forum litigation practice in under five years — an unusually rapid trajectory driven by consistent courtroom presence and rigorous legal preparation.

Utkarsh practises from the Chambers of Mr. Abhikalp Pratap Singh, Advocate on Record, Supreme Court of India, New Delhi. He appears regularly before the Supreme Court representing private individuals, corporations, and state governments — including the States of Maharashtra and Meghalaya — across civil, constitutional and commercial matters.

His arbitration practice is among the most distinctive aspects of his work. He has been briefed on ICC international arbitrations and statutory arbitrations conducted before tribunals of the highest composition, including proceedings presided over by former Chief Justices of India. His infrastructure arbitration work, particularly in the road and energy sectors on behalf of national PSUs, has given him deep familiarity with complex, high-stakes multi-party proceedings.

Prior to founding Amagi Legal, Utkarsh worked with the Chambers of the Additional Advocate General of Uttar Pradesh at the Allahabad High Court — an experience that built a broad and practical litigation base across civil, service, constitutional and criminal matters in one of India's busiest High Courts.

He has represented leading public institutions including NHAI, GAIL, NMDC, AAI, RBI, HDFC, IIFCL and IIM Lucknow, as well as private corporations and individuals across the full range of civil and criminal litigation. His work in service law — representing government employees, pensioners and defence personnel before the High Courts — reflects a commitment to accessible representation regardless of the size of the matter.

As a law student, Utkarsh competed in national and international moot court competitions, achieving a global ranking at the Herbert Smith Freehills Competition Law Moot at King's College London and winning the National Corporate Law Moot at NLIU Bhopal. These early experiences cemented a research ethic and a competitive instinct that continues to define his practice.
